Our Data

For this project, we required data from a variety of sources in order to identify ideal locations to install solar panels in South East Alberta. The below table contains the data we use in this project.

Data Sourced From Description Reason for Using
Annual Solar Radiation Alberta Government Open Data A layer file that indicates the average annual solar radiation in Alberta from 1971-2000 based on lattitude and other factors like cloud coverage It is essential to build a solar panel farm in an area that maximizes the collected energy from the sun
Countour Elevation Alberta Government Open Data A layer file with 50m contour intervals of topographic elevation data in Alberta Solar panels are the most effective when facing directly South. This data will be used to removee areas that are North sloped and prioritize South facing slopes
Forested Areas Alberta Government Open Data A layer file that indicates which areas in Alberta have tree coverage Solar panels take up a lot of space, something to be mindful of when doing that is minimizing the amount of deforestation during construction
Wildlife Habitat Sensitivity Alberta Government Open Data A layer file that assigns a value on a scale to individual points in Alberta based on how likely it is that an animal species at risk inhabits the area or if it is a legally protected area Solar panels take up a lot of space, something to be mindful of is to minimize the impact the farm would have on wildlife in Alberta
Power Line Locations AESO (Alberta Electric System Operator) A layer file that shows the major power lines in Southern and Eastern Alberta To construct a solar panel farm, it will be more realistic if high power infrastructure already exists in the surrounding area to minimize startup costs, this includes having nearby powerlines
Energy Transmission Capability AESO (Alberta Electric System Operator) A layer file that shows the amount of energy generation capcity that could be added at different substations in Southern and Eastern Alberta To construct a solar panel farm, it will be more realistic if high power infrastructure already exists in the surrounding area to minimize startup costs, this includes having energy infrastructure that can handle the increased load
