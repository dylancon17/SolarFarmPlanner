Progress Updates and Plan

Below is a log of our progress and planning at key points in our project

Team Members: Dylan Conley & Andrea Gail Tampus

November 4 Update:

ArcGIS:

We located the data we wanted to use in our project and ensured it properly loaded into ArcGIS. The data can be viewed under the data webpage.

Website

We created general templates to use in our website. We created 3 pages, our progress, the problem and data. Our website is being designed to be dynamically sized and mobile responsive.

Plan

The direct next steps will be to import our data fully into an ArcGis project and start manipulating it. We will then add our results to the webpage and fully document the data.

The steps we need to take in ArcGIS are:

  • Convert our contour into an aspect raster to eliminate north facing slopes from selection
  • Set a buffer around power lines that have a high increased power capability to find areas only surrounding those lines
  • Perform raster calculations to merge datasets together to create an overall score for solar panel compatibility factoring in sunshine, influence on wildlife and forestry
  • Filter out areas not in our area of study or areas not compatible for solar panels
  • Query only areas of a large enough area that are high quality locations for solar panels

November 18 Update:

ArcGIS:

We successfully performed and automated the following data processes:

  • Convert our DEM data into an aspect raster and reclassify its values such that South is equal to 1 and North is 0.
  • Convert our ecological zone data into raster and reclassify its value such that the minimum is 0 (not ideal) and maximum is 1 (ideal).
  • Set a buffer around power lines with high power capability.
  • Created thiessen polygon's surrounding power substations. Associated each polygon with a value between 0 and 1 with 1 being a high ability to transmit more power and converted to raster data.

We are still in the process of calculating the annual solar radiation in the areas we are interested as it is a time consuming task due to the size of the data.

Plan
